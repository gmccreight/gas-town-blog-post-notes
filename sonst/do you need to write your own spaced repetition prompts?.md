There is a notion that [[writing your own prompts may be more beneficial than using canned ones]].  

There has been pushback on that idea from some serious practitioners, though.  In [[t Dwarkesh custom spaced repetition card creation tooling]], [[person - Dwarkesh Patel]] feels that the process of creating the cards is often rote.

In [[my hacker news comment about canned prompts]] I detail [[person - Andy Matuschak]] and [[person - Michael Nielsen]]'s  evolving thoughts on the topic, where they seem to think that the process of generating high quality prompts is *so hard to do well*, that a person could benefit from having an expert create the cards for them.

Most people write sub-optimal prompts.  You see them everywhere in the wild.  Writing effective ones is really hard.  See [[Andy's blog post about writing SRS prompts]] for the *myriad* considerations.  Even just keeping [[The lenses mentioned in Andy Matuschak's "prompts" blog post|the lenses]] in mind while creating prompts is very hard.
