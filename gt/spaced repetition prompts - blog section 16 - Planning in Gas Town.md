## [[Blog Post -- Section 16 - Planning in Gas Town]]


Aside from keeping it on the rails, what is the hardest problem in Gas Town?
?
Keeping it fed — it churns through implementation plans so fast that you need a LOT of design and planning to keep the engine supplied with work.
<!--GUID:a4f7c92e1b306-->

<!--MEADOW_SR_GUID:6cac3f79c6386-->



What distinguishes [[Polecat type -- shiny|shiny]] and [[Polecat type -- chrome|chrome]] [[Term -- Polecats|polecat]] workflows from regular ones?
?
They add extra [[code review]] and testing steps, which makes them take longer to complete.
<!--GUID:b8d2e61f4a917-->

<!--MEADOW_SR_GUID:8f178396b0512-->



How can you turn an external plan (e.g. from Spec Kit or BMAD) into Gas Town work?
?
Ask an [[Term -- Agent|agent]] to convert it into [[project - Beads|Beads]] [[Term -- Epic|epics]]. For large plans, [[Term -- Swarm|swarm]] it and produce epics in a [[Term -- Convoy|convoy]].
<!--GUID:c3e9f50d72a48-->

<!--MEADOW_SR_GUID:a1e27757e1588-->



What is [[person -- Jeffrey Emanuel]]'s [[Rule of Five]]?
?
The observation that if you make an LLM review something five times, with different focus areas each time, it produces superior outcomes. The implementation counts as the first review.

Once work [[Term -- Molecule|molecules]] are generated, what makes it possible to burn through them autonomously?
?
You [[Term -- Hook|hook]] the work to a worker and Gas Town burns through it via [[Term -- Gastown Universal Propulsion Principle - GUPP|GUPP]] — no further intervention needed.
